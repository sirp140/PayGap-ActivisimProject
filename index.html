Gender Pay Gap

Equal work and pay for all.

What is it?

The gender pay gap refers to the disparity in earnings between men and women in the workplace. Despite progress in recent years, women still earn less than men on average for the same work. The gender pay gap is a complex issue that can have long-term effects on women's financial security and career advancement. Addressing this gap requires a collective effort from employers, policymakers, and individuals to ensure that everyone is paid fairly and equally, regardless of gender.

Why does it matter?

The gender pay gap affects women in the workforce. It represents a fundamental inequality between men and women, with women typically earning less than their male counterparts for performing the same job. This not only affects women's ability to support themselves and their families financially. Additionally, the gender pay gap perpetuates social injustice and reinforces gender discrimination in the workplace.

How to close it?

Closing the gender pay gap is essential for achieving gender equality, promoting economic growth, and creating a more just and equitable society for all.
